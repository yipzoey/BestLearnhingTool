Learning Objective 1: Preparing Desserts and Pastries

Skills

  • Classify hot and cold desserts
  • Identify the three main types of which gelatine strength is measured

Types of Desserts

What is a dessert? A dessert is a dish that is traditionally eaten at the end of a meal. It can be a savoury dish, but it is more often a sweetened dish typically containing fruits, dairy, chocolate or sugar. Hot desserts are served warm, either immediately after having been cooked, or having been warmed prior to service. Cold desserts are served at room temperature or below. They are typically set desserts, fixed through cooking, freezing, chilling, or the use of setting agents such as gelatine, agar agar or pectin.

Gelatines

Gelatine is used to set a dessert that is to be served at room temperature or chilled. It is most famously the setting agent used in jelly. Gelatine is graded by its Bloom strength. This is normally graded as bronze, silver or gold - bronze being the lowest Bloom value and gold being the highest. The Bloom value refers to the firmness or strength of the set gel, so a gold gelatine will give a firmer set than an equal quantity of bronze or silver gelatine.
